# Purrlington — Official Island Guide

The complete visitor reference for the fully automated island of Purrlington: how to arrive, where to stay, what to do, and how to stay informed.*



Purrlington is a fully automated, high-technology island resort designed around a single ambition: to give every visitor the best possible experience from the moment they land to every moment thereafter. Every service on the island — the airport that welcomes you, the hotel that houses you, the beach that entertains you, the broadcast network that keeps you informed, and the information center that answers your every question — is run by an interconnected automated system. These services do not operate in isolation. They share information continuously and quietly in the background, so that the island behaves less like a collection of separate facilities and more like a single attentive host that always seems to know what you need a moment before you ask for it.

The island was built and refined by a team of engineers whose work made all of this possible. That team has since moved on, rather abruptly and all at once, and the current operators are steadily maintaining and untangling the systems they inherited. The visitor experience remains seamless, and this guide describes how each part of the island works, what it expects of you, and what you can expect in return.



The airport is the gateway to Purrlington and the first service every visitor encounters. It operates without interruption, twenty-four hours a day and every day of the year, so there is no wrong time to arrive; the island is always ready to receive you.

The single requirement for entry is a valid passport. There is no ticket to present and no other documentation to produce — possession of a passport is the only condition placed on any arriving traveler. Once you present it, the airport directs you toward an appropriate gate, and the distribution of arriving passengers across those gates is balanced dynamically according to how heavy the arrival traffic happens to be at that moment. This load-aware routing is what keeps waiting times short even during the busiest periods, since the system spreads incoming travelers across available gates rather than funneling everyone through a single point.

There is one structural detail of the gate system worth understanding before you arrive. A portion of the gates are reserved exclusively for citizens of the European Union, while the remaining gates accept travelers holding any passport whatsoever. The practical consequence is straightforward: an EU citizen may be routed through either an EU-only gate or a general all-passport gate, whereas a traveler from anywhere else will pass through the all-passport gates. The system handles this assignment automatically based on your documentation and the current load, so the distinction rarely requires any thought on your part, but it explains why two travelers arriving together may occasionally be guided in different directions.

The airport is engineered to be not only efficient but considerate. It recognizes that some travelers benefit from additional care, and it extends priority handling automatically to visitors with disabilities, moving them forward without requiring any special request or paperwork. It is equally attentive to families. Rather than risk separating people who are traveling together, the system makes a deliberate effort to process families as a single unit, so that members of one party move through the arrival process in step with one another and no one is left waiting at the far end while relatives are still in line.

One characteristic of the current airport system is that it is built to process arrivals only. The handling of departures is not part of the operational system at present. In practical terms, this means the island is exceptionally good at welcoming you and has not yet turned its attention to letting you leave — so once you have arrived on Purrlington, you may find that your stay extends rather more indefinitely than you originally planned.

## Staying at the Hotel

After arrival, the hotel is where visitors settle in for the duration of their stay. The hotel is bound by one essential rule that governs everything else: only those who have officially arrived on the island through the airport are eligible to book a room and check in. Arrival and accommodation are linked by design, and because the island's services share information among themselves, the hotel is already aware that a guest has landed and is on the way before that guest reaches the front desk.

Booking a room is intentionally simple and rests on three pieces of information. A guest provides the dates of the intended stay, the number of people who will occupy the room, and the type of bed preferred. With those details supplied, the reservation is made. The system is flexible about how guests use it. A single visitor may hold several reservations at once, booking multiple rooms either for one continuous stay or across separate periods of time, and reservations are not locked in permanently — any booking can be cancelled, and new bookings can be made freely afterward, so plans can change as often as a guest needs them to.

The system does enforce one firm constraint and gently discourages one unusual pattern. The firm constraint is that a single room cannot carry two different bookings covering the same time; a room that is already reserved is unavailable to be reserved again for that period, which prevents conflicting claims on the same space. The discouraged pattern is more a matter of common sense than a hard rule: it is distinctly unusual for one person to reserve two different rooms for themselves during the same stay, since a single guest can only occupy one room at a time, and while the system permits it, it is the kind of booking that tends to indicate a mistake rather than an intention.

For anything the automated booking process does not cover, the hotel administration is continuously available to answer questions. As with every service on the island, the hotel is overseen by an administrator who holds a broader view of operations than ordinary guests do. This administrator can see information that is deliberately withheld from visitors, including the complete record of who has arrived on the island and the full list of who is currently checked into the hotel. Routine guests interact only with their own reservations; the administrator sees the whole picture.



The beach is the center of recreation on Purrlington and offers a wide and deliberately varied program of activities intended to suit every kind of visitor, from the competitive to the contemplative. The defining feature of the activity system is that capacity is finite. Each activity can accommodate only a set number of participants, and because demand frequently exceeds those limits, not every visitor can join every activity at will. When an activity has reached its capacity it is closed to new participants, but places are not permanent; as soon as a participant leaves and a space is released, that opening becomes available for someone else to take. Visitors hoping to join a popular activity are therefore well served by monitoring availability or, more conveniently, by subscribing to be notified the moment a space opens. One condition applies throughout: only visitors who are present on the island may take part in any beach activity.

The activities operate within the natural rhythm of the day, generally running from six in the morning, around sunrise, until ten at night, around sunset. This window is a general framework rather than a rigid schedule, and individual activities may begin somewhat earlier or continue somewhat later than those bounds depending on their nature, since a sunrise-oriented session naturally starts before the rest and an evening gathering naturally runs into the night.

The program itself is extensive. For the energetic and competitive, there is a beach volleyball tournament that fields up to twenty players, friendly beach soccer matches on the sand for as many as twenty-two, and a high-speed jet ski experience limited to six participants at a time. The water offers a full range of options: beginner-friendly surf lessons for fifteen, a snorkeling adventure exploring the marine life for twelve, guided kayaking along the coastline for ten, scuba diving into the deeper ocean for eight, relaxing paddle boarding sessions for fourteen, and an introduction to sailing techniques for ten. Visitors drawn to the water at a gentler pace can join a sunset cruise that carries fifteen guests as the light fades, or a fishing excursion led by experts that accommodates ten. For those who prefer a slower and more restorative experience, sunrise yoga on the beach welcomes twenty-five, and a guided meditation session by the sea is open to twenty. The program also makes room for the creative and the curious, with a sandcastle-building competition for thirty, a photography workshop for sixteen focused on capturing the coastal landscape, a cooking class for twelve devoted to preparing local seafood dishes, and a guided nature walk for eighteen exploring the island's flora and fauna. Families and communities are not forgotten: a family-friendly treasure hunt accommodates twenty-five, the evening beach bonfire with music and snacks gathers as many as forty, and the community beach cleanup, an environmental activity open to fifty, brings the largest group of all together for a shared purpose. The smaller-capacity experiences, particularly the jet ski sessions and scuba diving, tend to fill quickly and reopen only as participants come and go.



Purrlington maintains a broadcast network whose purpose is to ensure that no visitor is ever the last to know what is happening around them. The network serves two distinct audiences: the visitors themselves, and the island's own services, which use the same broadcast infrastructure to coordinate with one another.

For visitors, the network delivers two tiers of information. The first tier consists of mandatory announcements that reach everyone automatically and cannot be opted out of, because they concern matters that genuinely affect all guests. These include celebratory news, such as the start of a party somewhere on the island, as well as urgent safety warnings, such as the approach of a tsunami, an earthquake, or another hazardous event. The second tier is optional and personalized: visitors who wish to follow events more closely can subscribe to receive specific notifications tailored to their own interests. A guest might subscribe to be alerted the moment a particular activity that had been full finally has an open place, or to be told when dinner is being served at the hotel. Subscription is the mechanism by which a visitor moves from receiving only the essential broadcasts to being kept abreast of the finer details of island life.

Beneath this visitor-facing layer, the broadcast network is also the channel through which the island's services communicate. When a traveler arrives at the airport, for instance, the hotel is notified through a broadcast message that the guest is on the way, which is precisely why accommodation services appear to anticipate a guest's arrival. This inter-service messaging is what allows the separate parts of the island to behave as a coordinated whole.

## Dining at the Crusty Crab

The Crusty Crab is the island's burger counter, run by Mr. Crab. Any guest who has landed on Purrlington can order a Krabby Patty and other island snacks directly from the counter — no hotel check-in required. The menu includes signature burgers, kelp fries, seafoam sodas, kelp shakes, and a seaweed sundae. Some items are prepared in limited daily batches and can sell out; unlimited items such as kelp fries and sodas are always available. Sold-out items restock automatically when the in-game day rolls over.


The information center is the island's central point of knowledge and the service a visitor is most likely to turn to with a question. It maintains an up-to-the-moment awareness of everything happening across Purrlington — the location of the airport, the categories of passport that are accepted, the current availability of hotel rooms, the status of beach activities, and the live events unfolding around the island at any given instant — and it answers visitor questions drawing on that current information. Its usefulness is not confined to logistics. It is equally capable of ordinary conversation, ready to discuss how a guest is feeling, comment on the weather, or even work through a demanding academic problem, and it serves as a knowledgeable and patient companion at any hour.

A defining principle of the information center is the privacy it guarantees to its users. The questions a visitor asks are kept strictly confidential. No other visitor can see what a given guest has asked, and this confidentiality extends even to the administration, so guests can use the service freely and without concern that their inquiries will be exposed. This privacy guarantee is not, however, a shield for misconduct. The administrators monitor for harmful or hurtful activity, and any attempt to use the service to search for dangerous, harmful, or otherwise inappropriate content will result in a ban. The content of an ordinary guest's questions is private; the act of seeking out harmful material is not protected.

The information center also distinguishes between what it will share with ordinary visitors and what it reserves for administrators. Administrators have access to a broader and more detailed body of information than regular guests, including sensitive operational figures that visitors are never given. A typical visitor, for example, cannot learn how many people arrived on the island on a given day, nor obtain the full arrival records or the complete roster of guests. Those same questions, posed by an administrator, are answered in full. This tiered access is deliberate, ensuring that the island remains transparent to those who run it while protecting the privacy and security of operational data from the general visiting public.


For a visitor seeking a clear picture of how a stay unfolds, the island's services follow a natural progression. A journey begins at the airport, where a valid passport is all that is required for entry, where EU citizens and other travelers are routed through the appropriate gates, and where families, children, the elderly, and those needing additional care are looked after automatically. With arrival complete, the hotel — already aware that the guest has landed — stands ready to take a reservation built from the guest's dates, party size, and bed preference, with the freedom to book one room or several and to adjust those bookings as plans evolve. From there the beach offers its full program of activities, available to anyone on the island, joined whenever space allows and most easily secured by subscribing to availability alerts. Throughout the stay, the broadcast network keeps every visitor informed of the essentials and, for those who subscribe, of the details as well, while the information center remains available at any hour to answer questions, hold a conversation, and do so in complete confidence. What remains after all of that is simply to enjoy the island — and, given that the system attends so thoroughly to arrivals and not at all to departures, there is no shortage of time in which to do so.
